Anaphora of Mar Pathros

St. Peter, the Head of the Apostles

The Sign of Peace

Prayer of Peace

P:

O Lord, God of peace and Master of tranquility, make us all worthy to kiss one another with the kiss that does not depart from your love, and the embrace that is without deceit, that we might offer praise...

C:

Amen

P:

Peace be with you all.

C:

And with your spirit

D:

By the love of our Lord God, let us all give peace among one another with the Holy and Divine kiss.

Give the Kiss of Peace to one another. Afterwards, the congregation recites with bowed heads:

C:

Make us worthy, O Lord God, of this peace all the days of our lives. O our gracious Lord and God, having received this holy and divine peace, we bow our heads in your presence.

P:

Before you, O Lord God, the necks of our spirits and bodies are bowed, that we might receive blessings and help from you for the sake of our weakness. You are the help and refuge of all things, therefore we bear praise...

C:

Amen

P:

Shine your holy face over us, O Lord. Save us from every enemy who hates us. Wipe away and forgive all our many trespasses. We bear praise...

C:

Amen.

D:

Beloved brothers and sisters let us attend to this Holy Qurbana which is now offered before us by the hand of the priest, with commitment, reverence, humility, purity of heart, love, true faith and devotion. To God the Father, to whom all things belong, is offered this sacrifice of grace, peace and thanksgiving in a spirit of unity and concord by the priest for us all.

C:

This Qurbana is blessing, peace, and thanksgiving

The secret prayer of the priest as he removes the Shosappa from the Casa and the Pilasa

P:

This is the flintstone from which poured streams of water for the twelve tribes of Israel. This is the stone which was placed above the tomb of our Saviour.

The First Blessing (Rushma)

P:

The love of God the Father ✠ the grace of the only begotten Son ✠ and the communion and abiding presence of the Holy Spirit ✠ be with you all, dearly beloved, forever.

C:

And with your spirit.

The Dialogue

(The priest with uplifted arms)

P:

At this time, our thoughts, our hearts, and our mind must be in the heights at the right hand of God the Father, where the Messiah dwells.

C:

Truly they are with the Lord.

P:

We must give thanks to the Lord with fear and trembling

C:

It is good and right give thanks to the Lord with fear

The priest bows his head and prays in secret:

P:

Truly it is good and right for us to give thanks and to honor the Creator of those above, of those below, and of all creatures.

Sanctus

Raising his voice

P:

O Lord, the hosts of Angels and Archangels glorify you. And by voices of glory we too cry out "Holy Holy Holy art thou, Lord Almighty"

C:

Holy, Holy, Holy, Lord God Almighty
Thy praises fill heaven and earth
Hosanna in the highest!

Blessed is the one who has come
and is to come in the name of the Lord
Hosanna in the highest!

The priest bows his head and prays in secret:

P:

Thou art the Lord, Holy and rich in blessings. By your love of mankind, you sent your Son to take flesh from the Pure Virgin Mary for our salvation.

Words of Institution

Raising his voice

P:

>When He desired to taste death for our sake and to fulfill the Passover, He took bread in his Holy hands, blessed it, sanctified it, broke it, and gave it to the gathering of the Apostles, saying "Eat of this, for the forgiveness of sins and lives eternal".

C:

Amen

P:

In the same way, he took the cup, mixed from wine and water, blessed it, sanctified it, and gave it to his Holy Apostles, saying "Drink from this, all of you, for the pardon of sins and eternal lives"

C:

Amen

P:

He ordered them, "As often as you make this mystery, remember my death and resurrection until I come"

C:

O Lord, we remember Your death. We celebrate your resurrection. And we look forward to Your second coming. May your blessings be upon us all.

C:

Amen

Anamnesis

P:

C:

Have mercy upon us, O God the omnipotent Father. O Lord God, we praise you, we honor you, we glorify you. O Good One, we appeal to you that you might have mercy on us.

Epiclesis

D:

My beloved, how solemn is this moment and how terrible this hour, in which the Spirit of Holiness and Life descends in majesty from the highest heavens and abideth upon the holy mystery placed before us; and sanctifieth it. Let us all stand with fear and reverence and pray that peace may be with us and good will towards us all

C:

May peace be with us and tranquillity to all of us

P:

(In secret) Have mercy on us, O Lord, Have mercy upon us, and send upon us and upon this offering Your Holy Spirit, sanctify this mystery and help me and justify me.

P:

(Raising his voice) Answer unto me, O Lord. Answer unto me, O Lord. Answer unto me, O Lord. By Your grace, have mercy upon us.

C:

Kurie-elaison Kurie-elaison Kurie-elaison

P:

May the Holy Spirit descend and make this bread the body of our Lord Jesus Christ.

C:

Amen.

P:

May the Holy Spirit descend and make this cup the blood of our Lord Jesus Christ.

C:

Amen.

P:

O Lord, sanctify the bodies and souls of those who partake of these, that they may be for the pardon of debts. By these may they become heirs of eternal life

C:

Amen.

The Great Intercession

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

The Second Blessing (Rushma)

P:

The blessing of our great God and Savior Jesus Christ ✠ be with you all, forever.

C:

And with your spirit.

The Breaking of the Bread

The preist breaks the bread and blesses it. Then he prays in secret:

P:

O Lord God the Messiah, in Jerusalem...

Raising his voice

P:

C:

Our Father who art in heaven...

P:

C:

Amen. Before we recieve these holy mysteries offered before us, O merciful Lord God, we bow our heads in your presence.

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

P:

C:

Amen

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

The Third Blessing (Rushma)

P:

May the grace, mercy and blessings of the Holy and Glorious Trinity, uncreated, ✠ self-existent, eternal, ✠ adorable and one in essence ✠ be with you all dearly beloved forever.

C:

And with your Spirit. O Holy and Glorious Trinity, have mercy upon us.

Here follow the normal liturgy from "Let us hold ourselves in reverance and awe" until the Prayer of Thanksgiving

Prayer of Thanksgiving

P:

C:

Amen. After having received these Holy and Divine Mysteries, that have been given, let us again bow down our heads before the merciful Lord.

P:

C:

Amen

P:

Recite the normal liturgy from the Huthama to the end
