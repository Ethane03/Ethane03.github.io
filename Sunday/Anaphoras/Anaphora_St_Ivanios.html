Anaphora of Mar Ivanios

John Chrystosom, Archbishop of Constantinople (AD 440)

The Sign of Peace**

Prayer of Peace

P:

O Lord God, great and eternal, who is tranquility, peace, love, and a sea of mercies, by your grace may you bring peace to your holy church and guard the world. We bear praise and thanksgiving to you, your only son, and your holy spirit, hasha...

C:

Amen

P:

Peace be with you all.

C:

And with your spirit

D:

By the love of our Lord God, let us all give peace among one another with the Holy and Divine kiss.

Give the Kiss of Peace to one another. Afterwards, the congregation recites with bowed heads:

C:

Make us worthy, O Lord God, of this peace all the days of our lives. O our gracious Lord and God, having received this holy and divine peace, we bow our heads in your presence.

P:

O Lord, stretch out your merciful right hand and bless these your servants and worshippers who now bow their heads in your presence, that they may offer...

C:

Amen

P:

O Lord, empower us to offer this spiritual and bloodless sacrifice for the sake of your people's sins and wrongs, and may you abolish all our errors, and to you we offer...

C:

Amen.

D:

Beloved brothers and sisters let us attend to this Holy Qurbana which is now offered before us by the hand of the priest, with commitment, reverence, humility, purity of heart, love, true faith and devotion. To God the Father, to whom all things belong, is offered this sacrifice of grace, peace and thanksgiving in a spirit of unity and concord by the priest for us all.

C:

This Qurbana is blessing, peace, and thanksgiving

The secret prayer of the priest as he removes the Shosappa from the Casa and the Pilasa

P:

This is the flintstone from which poured streams of water for the twelve tribes of Israel. This is the stone which was placed above the tomb of our Saviour.

The First Blessing (Rushma)

P:

The love of God the Father ✠ the grace of the only begotten Son ✠ and the communion and abiding presence of the Holy Spirit ✠ be with you all, dearly beloved, forever.

C:

And with your spirit.

The Dialogue

(The priest with uplifted arms)

P:

At this time, our thoughts, our hearts, and our mind must be in the heights at the right hand of God the Father, where the Messiah dwells.

C:

Truly they are with the Lord.

P:

It is good and right that all thanksgiving be to you, God the Father, King of Kings, with the Son and the Spirit.

C:

Assuredly it is good and right

Sanctus

P:

All the orders, the companies and the heavenly hosts: angels, archangels, cherubim, seraphim, thrones and dominions, invisible and innumerable, unceasingly praise and glorify and with incessant mouths and unspeakable voices they sing the hymn of victory and cry out and say:

C:

Holy, Holy, Holy, Lord God Almighty
Thy praises fill heaven and earth
Hosanna in the highest!

Blessed is the one who has come
and is to come in the name of the Lord
Hosanna in the highest!

The priest bows his head and prays in secret:

P:

Truly, You are holy God the Father with Your Holy Spirit Who are continually hallowed with Your Holy Son our Lord Jesus Christ

Words of Institution

Raising his voice

P:

He Who, on our account, through His love took flesh and for the sake of our salvation He, of His own will, emptied Himself and suffered in the flesh and on the night of His Passion, He took bread in His holy hand, He blessed + +, He sanctified +, he broke and gave His disciples, and said: Take eat of it, this is My Body which is given for the remission of debts, the forgiveness of sins and for everlasting life.

C:

Amen

P:

Likewise, He took the cup, He blessed + +, He sanctified + and gave His disciples, saying: Take, drink of it all of you, this is my blood poured out for the remission of debts, the forgiveness of sins and for everlasting life.

C:

Amen

P:

He told them in advance and commanded them, saying: Whenever you enjoy this Holy Mystery, make remembrance of My death and My resurrection until My coming.

C:

O Lord, we remember Your death. We celebrate your resurrection. And we look forward to Your second coming. May your blessings be upon us all.

C:

Amen

Anamnesis

P:

Though we are Your sinful and guilty servants, we commemorate all Your redemptive dispensation as well as Your fearful and glorious second coming of which we tremble and fear; we offer You petition and supplication, and through You we entreat Your Father, and say:

C:

Have mercy upon us, O God the omnipotent Father. O Lord God, we praise you, we honor you, we glorify you. O Good One, we appeal to you that you might have mercy on us.

Epiclesis

D:

My beloved, how solemn is this moment and how terrible this hour, in which the Spirit of Holiness and Life descends in majesty from the highest heavens and abideth upon the holy mystery placed before us; and sanctifieth it. Let us all stand with fear and reverence and pray that peace may be with us and good will towards us all

C:

May peace be with us and tranquillity to all of us

P:

(In secret) Send down, O Lord, Your Holy Spirit from Your dwelling place, and may He descend and rest upon this bread and this cup which is here set forth that He may sanctify them and make me pure and spotless.

P:

(Raising his voice) Answer unto me, O Lord. Answer unto me, O Lord. Answer unto me, O Lord. By Your grace, have mercy upon us.

C:

Kurie-elaison Kurie-elaison Kurie-elaison

P:

May the Holy Spirit descend and sanctify this bread that it may be the body of our Lord Jesus Christ.

C:

Amen.

P:

May the Holy Spirit descend and sanctify this cup which is prepared that it may be the blood of our Lord Jesus Christ.

C:

Amen.

P:

O Lord, sanctify the bodies and souls of those who partake of these, that they may be for the pardon of debts. By these may they become heirs of eternal life

C:

Amen.

The Great Intercession

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

The Second Blessing (Rushma)

P:

The blessing of our great God and Savior Jesus Christ ✠ be with you all, forever.

C:

And with your spirit.

The Breaking of the Bread

The preist breaks the bread and blesses it. Then he prays in secret:

P:

O Lord God the Messiah, in Jerusalem...

Raising his voice

P:

O Great and Powerful God, Who, through Your love toward mankind, called us and brought us near this Divine Gift through which we glorify You, O Father, and worship the Holy Spirit, and we sing the Lord's Prayer of Your Only-begotten Son where with clearness of mind, purity of thought, simplicity of tongue, cleanliness of body and a soul full of confidence we may dare to call upon You, O Heavenly, Almighty, Holy God, and pray and say: "Our Father Who art in heaven"

C:

Our Father who art in heaven...

P:

O Good and Merciful Father, Who made us worthy to be called Your children, deliver us from hard temptations, save us from the evil one and from all his powers and from the injuries of wicked men so that we may offer You praise and thanksgiving and to Your Only-begotten Son and to Your Holy Spirit, now, always and forever.

C:

Amen. Before we recieve these holy mysteries offered before us, O merciful Lord God, we bow our heads in your presence.

P:

O Great and Almighty King, under Whose scepter of sovereignty the whole creation bends down, send Your blessing to Your Holy Church which is now bent before You, that we may offer You praise and thanksgiving and to Your Only-begotten Son and to Your Holy Spirit, now, always and forever.

The Third Blessing (Rushma)

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

P:

May the grace, mercy and blessings of the Holy and Glorious Trinity, uncreated, ✠ self-existent, eternal, ✠ adorable and one in essence ✠ be with you all dearly beloved forever.

C:

And with your Spirit. O Holy and Glorious Trinity, have mercy upon us.

Here follow the normal liturgy from "Let us hold ourselves in reverance and awe" until the Prayer of Thanksgiving

Prayer of Thanksgiving

P:

We thank you, O God, and we praise Your Holy Name for making us, who are unworthy, to become worthy of this Divine Gift which is the Body and Blood of Your Only-begotten Son. We, therefore, offer You praise and thanksgiving and to Your Only-begotten Son and to Your Holy Spirit, now, always and forever

C:

Amen. After having received these Holy and Divine Mysteries, that have been given, let us again bow down our heads before the merciful Lord.

P:

Jesus Christ our God, Who of Your own will and that of Your Father and of Your Holy Spirit, were sacrificed on our behalf and gave us Your Holy Body to eat and Your Atoning Blood to drink, confirm us in Your love, fill us with Your fear, establish in us Your faith, adorn us with blameless manners and forgive our sins so that we may continually glorify You and God Your Father and Your Holy Spirit, now, always and forever.

C:

Amen

P:

Recite the normal liturgy from the Huthama to the end
