Anaphora of Mar Xystus

The Bishop of Rome (+AD 251)

The Sign of Peace

Prayer of Peace

P:

Grant thou, O Lord, tranquility, peace, and eternal love for your Church, your sheep, and your people forever.We bear praise and thanksgiving unto you, your only son, and your Holy Spirit, now and always and forever. Hasha...

C:

Amen

P:

Peace be with you all.

C:

And with your spirit

D:

By the love of our Lord God, let us all give peace among one another with the Holy and Divine kiss.

Give the Kiss of Peace to one another. Afterwards, the congregation recites with bowed heads:

C:

Make us worthy, O Lord God, of this peace all the days of our lives. O our gracious Lord and God, having received this holy and divine peace, we bow our heads in your presence.

P:

O Lord, may we all be blessed by you. Make us all worthy of the good end befitting all the people of peace. Make us worthy to do your holy will, and that of your only son, and your Holy Spirit, now and always and forever.

C:

Amen

P:

O Lord, by this Holy Qurbana that we offer to you, may these mysteries become for us the removal of all sinful passions and the intimacy of acts of righeousness.

C:

Amen.

D:

Beloved brothers and sisters let us attend to this Holy Qurbana which is now offered before us by the hand of the priest, with commitment, reverence, humility, purity of heart, love, true faith and devotion. To God the Father, to whom all things belong, is offered this sacrifice of grace, peace and thanksgiving in a spirit of unity and concord by the priest for us all.

C:

This Qurbana is blessings, peace, and thanksgiving

The secret prayer of the priest as he removes the Shosappa from the Casa and the Pilasa

P:

This is the flintstone from which poured streams of water for the twelve tribes of Israel. This is the stone which was placed above the tomb of our Saviour.

The First Blessing (Rushma)

P:

The love of God the Father ✠ the grace of the only begotten Son ✠ and the communion and abiding presence of the Holy Spirit ✠ be with you all, dearly beloved, forever.

C:

And with your spirit.

The Dialogue

(The priest with uplifted arms)

P:

At this time the thoughts hearts and minds of us all must be in the heights at the right hand of God the Father, where the Messiah dwells.

C:

Truly they are with the Lord.

P:

To sing praises and to worship the one true God, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, with heart, mind, and mouth

C:

Assuredly is good and right.

P:

Your faithful people, O Lord, are joined with the invisible hosts and innumerable ranks, praising your thrice as the Seraphim do, crying aloud:

C:

Holy, Holy, Holy, Lord God Almighty
Thy praises fill heaven and earth
Hosanna in the highest!

Blessed is the one who has come
and is to come in the name of the Lord
Hosanna in the highest!

The priest bows his head and prays in secret:

P:

Holy art thou, O God the Father, who by your son's incarnation saved the world from the way of error and from sin. Holy is your only son. Holy is your Spirit who is holier than all things.

Words of Institution

Raising his voice

P:

When he prepared for the salvific passion, He took bread in His Holy hands - blessed it - sanctified it - broke it - and divided it for the Holy Apostles. He gave it as his propitiatory body for life eternal.

C:

Amen

P:

He took the cup, mixed of wine and water - blessed it - sanctified it - and gave it to the Holy Apostles. He gave it as his propitiatory blood for life eterna

C:

Amen

P:

And with these He added this admonition, saying: So often as You partake of these, make remembrance of My death, My burial and My resurrection.

C:

O Lord, we remember Your death. We celebrate your resurrection. And we look forward to Your second coming. May your blessings be upon us all.

C:

Amen

Anamnesis

P:

O Lord, your Church forgets not the miraculous wonders of your savlific act, and also the fearful signs of your coming in which you will reward each one according to their works. Therefore, we your Church and your Flock pray to you, and by you to your Father

C:

Have mercy upon us, O God the omnipotent Father. O Lord God, we praise you, we honor you, we glorify you. O Good One, we appeal to you that you might have mercy on us.

C:

Amen

Epiclesis

D:

My beloved, how solemn is this moment and how terrible this hour, in which the Spirit of Holiness and Life descends in majesty from the highest heavens and abideth upon the holy mystery placed before us; and sanctifieth it. Let us all stand with fear and reverence and pray that peace may be with us and good will towards us all

C:

May peace be with us and tranquillity to all of us

P:

(In secret) Be gracious unto me, O Lord, be gracious unto me and unto your whole inheritance. Sanctify these offerings by the dwelling of the Holy Spirit who eternally proceeds from you and is substantially obtained from your Son

P:

(Raising his voice) Answer unto me, O Lord. Answer unto me, O Lord. Answer unto me, O Lord. By Your grace, have mercy upon us.

C:

Kurie-elaison Kurie-elaison Kurie-elaison

P:

May the Holy Spirit make this bread be the holy body of our God the Messiah.

C:

Amen.

P:

May the Holy Spirit make this cup be the holy blood of our God the Messiah

C:

Amen.

P:

O Lord, let us who partake of these be made joyous along with you, that we may offer You praise and thanksgiving and to Your Only-begotten Son and to Your Holy Spirit, now, always and forever.

C:

Amen.

P:

Be thou, O Lord, unto the shepherds a tongue unsubdued by the evil doctrines of heresy.

The Great Intercession

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

The Second Blessing (Rushma)

P:

The blessing of our great God and Savior Jesus Christ ✠ be with you all, forever.

C:

And with your spirit.

The Breaking of the Bread

The priest breaks the bread and blesses it. Then he prays in secret:

P:

O Lord God the Messiah, in Jerusalem...

Raising his voice

P:

O God the Father of Mercies, open our mouths and lips that we may raise our voice unto you and worship you, saying "Our Father who art in heaven". Sancify the bodies and souls of us all and purify our minds and hearts.

C:

Our Father who art in heaven...

P:

Change, O Lord, all the things that cause us harm and cause loss to those that do us good and benefit us that we may offer You praise and thanksgiving and to Your Only-begotten Son and to Your Holy Spirit, now, always and forever.

C:

Amen. Before we recieve these holy mysteries offered before us, O merciful Lord God, we bow our heads in your presence.

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

P:

O Lord, may all your garces, blessings, and good gifts come and be poured out over your church, over your sheep, and over your people, that we may offer...

C:

Amen

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

The Third Blessing (Rushma)

P:

May the grace, mercy and blessings of the Holy and Glorious Trinity, uncreated, ✠ self-existent, eternal, ✠ adorable and one in essence ✠ be with you all dearly beloved forever.

C:

And with your Spirit. O Holy and Glorious Trinity, have mercy upon us.

Here follow the normal liturgy from "Let us hold ourselves in reverance and awe" until the Prayer of Thanksgiving

Prayer of Thanksgiving

P:

The mouths that are accustomed to vegetable food give thanks to that grace by which we were made worthy of this Divine Gift which is the Body and the Blood of Your Only begotten Son, by Whom and with Whom is due to You glory, honor and dominion with Your Holy Spirit, now, always and forever.

C:

Amen. After having received these Holy and Divine Mysteries, that have been given, let us again bow down our heads before the merciful Lord.

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

P:

O heavenly Bread, the Christ Who condescended toward us that He might become imperishable food for us, make us not, on Your second coming, food for the unquenchable fire that we may offer You praise and thanksgiving and to Your Father and to Your Holy Spirit, now, always and forever.

C:

Amen

P:

Recite the normal liturgy from the Huthama to the end
