Praise be to you, O Self Existent One, hidden and far from all comprehension, thought, and mind, invisible, exalted, self-existent, self-knowing, self-sufficient, who created and sustains and orders all things seen and unseen, without beginning or end, one unsearchable existence, one equal authority, one united will, indescribable, and eternal, one true God, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Sedra

P:

O God, who is invisible and inscrutible, you are one in essence, apprehensible in three holy persons, One in three, a perfect trinity in three perfect persons, one perfect self-existence, one eternal essence, the true God, you are three perfect persons, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. O God our Father, to renew the man, Adam, whom you had created in your likeness, and who went astray into demon worship by the deceit of the Evil one, you decided to send your Son, the Word, from your bosom and majestic royal throne and when he had beome human without change, he made clear to us divine mysteries. He is from you, and he explained to us the hidden and mysterious things of your wisdom, so that we might know you, and he taught us about the third light of the Godhead, the person of the Holy Spirit, who proceeds from you, the Father, in an indescribable manner. He is the true spirit. He is the comforting spirit. He is the discerning spirit of wisdom. He is the allpowerful and perfect spirit. He is the immesurable Spirit, who loves mankind, who supported the prophets, and enlightened the apostles. He is the Spirit who armed the Martyrs and trained the Teachers; He is the teacher, the door of good works, the omnipotent, pure of heart, and the shepherd of all things. He has many kinds of gifts, and is the fountain-head of divine gifts, equal in essence to you and your only Son, who is equal upon the throne of your kingship, who spoke through the law, the prophets, and apostles, and is near to all things, and perfecter of all things. We pray to you, O Lord, on this great day of Pentecost on which he overshadowed the holy Apostles. Stretch our your right hand, full of mercy and compassion, over your servants who worship you. Fill each of us with the splendor of your divine gifts. Grant each of us the grace of your Holy Spirit. By his divine actions, wipe from us, the children of grace, all impurity and uncleanliness. Make us your holy abodes and homes for you to dwell in. Guard us, that we might not be subject to the opposition of our enemies nor be oppressed by them. Grant us spiritual strength, power, and aid to live out our lives in the true faith. For you ule over us, O God the Father, with your only begotten son, and your Spirit, who perfects all things, the Lord and Giver of life, equal to you in essence, hosho...

O Lord, God and Master of all Mercies, again we fall to our knees, and pray to you, and give thanks to you. For when the disciples waited in Jerusalem until the day of Pentecost, fifty days from the day of that Holy Sunday of the Resurrection which gave life to your eternal son, like their Lord had promised, you filled them with your Holy Spirit with a sound of joy that appeared among them, and tongues of fire rested upon each of them so that they might praise you, the giver of all goodness, and that all who were living in Jerusalem, and all who were under heaven would be amazed and wonder O God the Father, who was hallowed, and proclaimed, and worshipped by them, you sanctified your holy apostles, so that they might bring mankind to knowledge of your divinity, by baptizing them your name and that of your only son and your holy spirit, and because of them, and with them, we too worship and give thanks and magnify One God, the holy Trinity, whom they proclaimed and confirmed, for all praise and honor and authority belong to you, amen.
