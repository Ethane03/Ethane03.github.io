Thuyaba

The Preparatory Service Before the Holy Qurbana

P:

Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit.

C:

Weak and sinful as we are, may his mercy and blessings be poured out upon us, in both worlds, forever.

P:

O Lord, merciful and lover of mankind! Make us worthy to stand before you with wisdom, devotion, proper spiritual preparation, purity, and holiness, and to serve you, the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, who holds the position of Creator and Lord to whom all things owe praise, now and forever, hosho vab’kula s’ban l’olmin.

C:

Amen

Psalm 51

The priest and the people should alternatingly chant Psalm 51

Have mercy on me, O God, in Your loving kindness; in the abundance of your mercy blot out my sins.

Wash me thoroughly from my wickedness, and cleanse me from my sin.

For I acknowledge my fault, and my sin is before me always.

Against you only have I sinned and done evil in your sight, that You may be justified in Your words, and vindicated in your judgement.

For I was born in guilt and in sin did my mother conceive me.

But You take pleasure in truth and You have made known to me the secrets of your wisdom.

Thou shalt purge me with hyssop, and I shall be clean; thou shalt wash me, and I shall be whiter than snow.

Thou shalt make me hear of joy and gladness, that the bones which thou hast broken may rejoice.

Turn thy face from my sins, and put out all my misdeeds.

Make me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit within me.

Cast me not away from thy presence, and take not thy holy Spirit from me.

O give me the comfort of thy help again, and establish me with thy free Spirit.

Then shall I teach thy ways unto the wicked, and sinners shall be converted unto thee.

Deliver me from blood-guiltiness, O God, thou that art the God of my health; and my tongue shall sing of thy righteousness.

Thou shalt open my lips, O Lord, and my mouth shall show thy praise.

For thou desirest no sacrifice, else would I give it thee; but thou delightest not in burnt-offerings.

The sacrifice of God is a troubled spirit; a broken and contrite heart, O God, shalt thou not despise.

O be favourable and gracious unto Sion; build thou the walls of Jerusalem.

Then shalt thou be pleased with the sacrifice of righteousness, with the burnt-offerings and oblations; then shall they offer young bullocks upon thine altar.

As the priest enters the madbaha (Psalm 43:4)

P:

I come to the altar of God, to the presence of God, who gladdens my youth.

As he stands before the altar and bows his head

P:

O God, I have entered your house, and I bow before your throne, O heavenly Father. Forgive me of every sin that I have committed against you.

As he kneels before the horns of the altar (Psalm 118: 27, 28)

P:

O Lord, bind our holy days with chains to the horns of your altar. You are our God, and we will exalt you. You are our God, and we will sing praise to you.

As the north candle is lit

In Your Light, we see the light, Jesus, full of light.
It’s Your Light that shines upon our world, light our minds.
May Your Light reveal the sin in our hearts.
As we humbly pray, in spirit and truth.

As the south candle is lit

Pure and holy One who dwells on high, in mansions of light.
Cleanse our hearts and souls; we lift them up, unto You.
Grant us purity of thought and mind,
That our prayers may rise up to You.

Promeon

P:

Let us all pray, and beseech the Lord for blessing and mercy

C:

O Lord of blessings, bless us and help us.

P:

May we be made worthy to endlessly bear praise and worship, honor and adoration, and glory that does not fade. Praise be to the One Father, the merciful one who answers unto the sinners who call upon his name, and to the One Son, the kind one who accepts the penitent who knock on his door, and to the One living and Holy Spirit, who gives forgiveness to the guilty who appeal to Him. Thou art worthy of praise and honor and glory both now and all the days of our lives.

C:

Amen

Sedra

P:

O God, who is peaceful, who is compassionate, who is meek, who loves mankind, who is pleased not in sacrifice but in mercy, who loves a contrite heart more than burnt offerings, and who accepts a humble spirit over the offering of sacrifices and the blood of bulls and fattened lambs. Accept now our spiritual sacrifice on your living altar. Make us worthy to offer ourselves by this living service to you as an acceptable sacrifice, a life pleasing to your name. May we offer unto you living spiritual sacrifices, on your High Altar with grieving hearts and humble spirits. Let us become unto you a pure and great flock, so that we may be made anew and be sent into the new world as new people, being gathered with the souls of the wise and learned, and holding the torches of the worthiness of faith. Make us all worthy to sing praises in your house to the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, hosho vab’kula s’ban l’olmin.

C:

Amen

Qolo

I knock at Your door and I beg for mercy
  From your treasure-house
I m a sinner who has strayed from Your way
Grant that I confess and be free from my sins
  And live in Your grace

O merciful king, who is praised and honored
by the rulers of the earth
We knock at your door - where have you gone?
Without your grace, who do we have to pray for us?

Father Son and Holy Spirit!
You are our fortress and protection
From the evil one and his armies against us.
Gather us under your wings of mercy
On the day of our resurrection.

O Lord, may the voice of our prayer
Open up the door of heaven
Let the angels in their ranks sing,
"Beautiful is the voice of mortals,
And the Lord shall grant their plea"
