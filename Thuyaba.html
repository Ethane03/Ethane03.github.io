Thuyaba

The Preparatory Service Before the Holy Qurbana

Part I: The Service of Melchizedek

P:

Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit.

C:

Weak and sinful as we are, may his mercy and blessings be poured out upon us, in both worlds, forever.

P:

O Lord, merciful and lover of mankind! Make us worthy to stand before you with wisdom, devotion, proper spiritual preparation, purity, and holiness, and to serve you, the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, who holds the position of Creator and Lord to whom all things owe praise, now and forever, hosho vab’kula s’ban l’olmin.

C:

Amen

Psalm 51

The priest and the people should alternatingly chant Psalm 51

Have mercy on me, O God, in Your loving kindness; in the abundance of your mercy blot out my sins.

Wash me thoroughly from my wickedness, and cleanse me from my sin.

For I acknowledge my fault, and my sin is before me always.

Against you only have I sinned and done evil in your sight, that You may be justified in Your words, and vindicated in your judgement.

For I was born in guilt and in sin did my mother conceive me.

But You take pleasure in truth and You have made known to me the secrets of your wisdom.

Thou shalt purge me with hyssop, and I shall be clean; thou shalt wash me, and I shall be whiter than snow.

Thou shalt make me hear of joy and gladness, that the bones which thou hast broken may rejoice.

Turn thy face from my sins, and put out all my misdeeds.

Make me a clean heart, O God, and renew a right spirit within me.

Cast me not away from thy presence, and take not thy holy Spirit from me.

O give me the comfort of thy help again, and establish me with thy free Spirit.

Then shall I teach thy ways unto the wicked, and sinners shall be converted unto thee.

Deliver me from blood-guiltiness, O God, thou that art the God of my health; and my tongue shall sing of thy righteousness.

Thou shalt open my lips, O Lord, and my mouth shall show thy praise.

For thou desirest no sacrifice, else would I give it thee; but thou delightest not in burnt-offerings.

The sacrifice of God is a troubled spirit; a broken and contrite heart, O God, shalt thou not despise.

O be favourable and gracious unto Sion; build thou the walls of Jerusalem.

Then shalt thou be pleased with the sacrifice of righteousness, with the burnt-offerings and oblations; then shall they offer young bullocks upon thine altar.

As the priest enters the madbaha (Psalm 43:4)

P:

I come to the altar of God, to the presence of God, who gladdens my youth.

As he stands before the altar and bows his head

P:

O God, I have entered your house, and I bow before your throne, O heavenly Father. Forgive me of every sin that I have committed against you.

As he kneels before the horns of the altar (Psalm 118: 27, 28)

P:

O Lord, bind our holy days with chains to the horns of your altar. You are my God, and I will exalt you. You are my God, and I will sing praise to you.

As the north candle is lit

In Your Light, we see the light, Jesus, full of light.
It’s Your Light that shines upon our world, light our minds.
May Your Light reveal the sin in our hearts.
As we humbly pray, in spirit and truth.

As the south candle is lit

Pure and holy One who dwells on high, in mansions of light.
Cleanse our hearts and souls; we lift them up, unto You.
Grant us purity of thought and mind,
That our prayers may rise up to You.

Promeon

P:

Let us all pray, and beseech the Lord for blessing and mercy

C:

O Lord of blessings, bless us and help us.

P:

May we be made worthy to endlessly bear praise and worship, honor and adoration, and glory that does not fade. Praise be to the One Father, the merciful one who answers unto the sinners who call upon his name, and to the One Son, the kind one who accepts the penitent who knock on his door, and to the One living and Holy Spirit, who gives forgiveness to the guilty who appeal to Him. Thou art worthy of praise and honor and glory both now and all the days of our lives.

C:

Amen

Sedra

P:

O God, who is peaceful, who is compassionate, who is meek, who loves mankind, who is pleased not in sacrifice but in mercy, who loves a contrite heart more than burnt offerings, and who accepts a humble spirit over the offering of sacrifices and the blood of bulls and fattened lambs. Accept now our spiritual sacrifice on your living altar. Make us worthy to offer ourselves by this living service to you as an acceptable sacrifice, a life pleasing to your name. May we offer unto you living spiritual sacrifices, on your High Altar with grieving hearts and humble spirits. Let us become unto you a pure and great flock, so that we may be made anew and be sent into the new world as new people, being gathered with the souls of the wise and learned, and holding the torches of the worthiness of faith. Make us all worthy to sing praises in your house to the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, hosho vab’kula s’ban l’olmin.

C:

Amen

Part II: The Service of Aaron

The Vesting of the Priest and Preperation of the Elements

P:

Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit.

C:

Weak and sinful as we are, may his mercy and blessings be poured out upon us, in both worlds, forever.

P:

Lord God! Cleanse our hearts from every evil conscience, and make us worthy to enter your great and high most holy place. O Father, Son, and Holy Spirit! May we ever stand with cleanliness and purity before your holy altar and offer living and spiritual sacrifices, both now and forever. hosho vab’kula s’ban l’olmin.

C:

Amen

(Prayer before the vesting of the celebrant)

P:

O Lord God, remove the unholy garments with which Satan has covered me because I had leaned towards sinful deeds, and clothe me O Lord my God, with dignified garments that will at all times be appropriate for the worship that glorifies You and brings majesty to Your holy name. Amen.

(Prayer when the hands are being washed)

P:

O Lord God, completely cleanse my evil conscience and make me worthy to enter Your great, exalted, lofty and holy place with a purified heart and to stand before Your holy altar in purity, holiness, and in true faith, to always offer spiritual sacrifices to You: O Father, Son and Holy Spirit. Amen.

(Prayer when putting on the white garment)

P:

O Lord God, by the power of the Holy Spirit, clothe me with an incorruptible robe. O God: the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit, graciously enable me to walk before You in true faith, and to be virtuous and pure, all the days of my life. Amen.

(Prayer when putting on the stole)

P:

You will equip me with strength for the battle. You will make those who stand in hostility against me, kneel in humility before me. Amen.

(Prayer when tying the girdle)

P:

O Almighty, gird me with Your sword. Your strength and majesty will always be victorious. Amen.

(Prayer when putting on the left sleeve)

P:

O God: the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit, make us chosen vessels for the worship of Your greatness, and holy temples that befit the majesty of Your holy name, and consecrate our bodies to become righteous weapons that are always willing to do good deeds for You. ‘He trains my hands for battle and strengthens my arms like a brass bow.’ Amen.

(Prayer when putting on the right sleeve)

P:

Your right hand will help me, and Your chastisement will help me mature. Amen.

(Prayer when putting on the cope)

P:

You clothe Your priests with justice and Your righteous ones with majesty. Do not turn the face of Your Anointed, because of Your servant David. Amen.

(Prayer when placing the bread on the paten)

P:

O Lord, You were led for slaughter like a lamb, and like a sheep before its shearer, You stood silently. You did not speak because of Your humility. O Lord, You have confirmed Your throne in holiness. Establish it with Your holy hands O Lord; “The Lord will reign forever!” Amen.

(Prayer when mixing the wine and the water in the chalice)

P:

O Lord Jesus Christ, You were crucified on a tree in Jerusalem between two robbers, and Your side was pierced with a spear. Blood and water flowed from You, to atone for the sins of all creation. The one who saw this is a witness and we also believe that his witness is true. What can I render to You O Lord, for all Your goodness to me? I will take the chalice of salvation and call on the name of the Lord. I will pay my vows to the Lord in the presence of all the people. O first-born of God the Father, accept the first-fruit from the hands of Your weak and sinful servant. Amen.

(The celebrant covers the paten and chalice with the kablana and kneels)

P:

O Lord God Almighty, who forgives the atrocities of humanity, and does not delight in the death of sinners, I lift my heart and hands and plead to You for the redemption of all my transgressions. Though I am unworthy to completely abide in You and to receive from You the divine gift of Your mysteries, I beseech You to protect my mind from the snares of adversaries, my eyes from covetousness, my ears from listening to vain things, my hands from despicable deeds, and protect me so that I may be used by You. Amen.

(The celebrant stands on the altar step and removes the cover of the patenand the chalice)

Psalm 93

P:

The Lord reigns. He is robed in majesty! The Lord wears strength and is strengthened. He set the world in order that it would not be moved. He sets Your throne in order from the first and You are from eternity. O Lord, the rivers arerisen, the rivers have raised their voice; the rivers are risen in purity. The storms of the sea are stronger than the voice of many waters; The Lord is glorious in the high places! Your testimonies are so very trustworthy; O Lord, holiness is fitting for Your house, forever!

O Lord our Messiah, the pure and spotless Lamb of God who offered Yourself as an acceptable sacrifice to the Father, for the remission of sins and the salvation of the whole world, make us worthy to offer ourselves to You as a living sacrifice that is pleasing to You and is likened to Your sacrifice for us.

Amen.

(The celebrant takes the paten in his right hand and the chalice in his lefthand and crosses his arms in the form of a cross by placing his right armover his left arm)

P:

Our Lord God and Saviour Jesus Christ, we recall Your salvific ministry, and in accordance with Your divine command, we meditate upon all that You did and suffered for us. We also especially remember the annunciation through the angel, Your glorious conception and birth in human form, Your baptism in the River Jordan, the forty-day fast, the salvific suffering and crucifixion, life-giving death, honourable burial, glorious resurrection, ascension into heaven and Your eternal presence at the right hand of God the Father. Amen.

(The chalice and paten are placed on the Thableetha and covered with the Sosappa)

P:

The glory of the majestic God fills the heavens. His majesty fills the whole of creation. Amen.

Preface (Promeon)

P:

Let us all pray to the Lord, and plead for His blessings andmercy.

C:

O Lord the source of all blessings, bless us and help us.

May we become worthy to offer You continuous praise, thanksgiving, glory, adoration and everlasting exaltation. O Lord the Messiah who lives and is the source of life and the glorious fruit of the virgin’s womb, who raises the dead with Your joyful voice and makes them rejoice with You in Your joyful majesty, You are worthy of praise, worship and honour, at this time and forever more. Amen.

Prayer (Sedra)

P:

O Lord, the Creator of the universe, the Sustainer of all creatures, the blessed shoot which sprang from Mary and filled the whole earth with Your magnificent fragrance, and whose glorious teachings removed the odour of ignorance from every quarter, we worship, thank and praise You. Just as Aaron, the priest at the tabernacle, averted the plague that affected the Israelites, by offering pure incense, we too offer our prayers as incense. O Lord God, accept the prayers for our sins and offences, that we Your humble servants offer to You. We will always offer praise and thanksgiving to You, to the Father and to the Holy Spirit, now and forever more.Amen.

Prayer of Incense (Ethra)

(The celebrant puts the incense in the censer, swings the censer over thepaten and chalice and prays)

P:

Glory be to the merciful Father. Glory be to the merciful Son. Glory be to the living and Holy Spirit. All you righteous, praise the Lord! All the nations of the earth, praise Him! Praise be to the Father, to the Son and to the Holy Spirit. As it was in the beginning, is now and forever shall be. Amen.

P:

O Lord in Your mercy, accept the worship of Your servant. Hear the prayers of Your priests. O Messiah, who is worshipped and praised along with the Father and the living Holy Spirit, we ask that You be always receptive to the service of those who worship You. Amen.

P:

O Lord our God, we ask that You remove all anger from Your people. Be merciful to the sheep of Your pasture. May Your reconciliation and peace be established throughout the entire world forever. Amen.

(The celebrant now recites the Kauma and the Nicene Creed in private)

The Thuyaba is completed
