Bathe D'Hasha

O Lord You did wash the feet of
the trecherous, evil Judas
Though you lovingly - wiped his feet clean
Not a speck of the wicked sin
inside his heart was washed away

Sanctify and teach your servants
that by external rituals
sins are not removed, nor are hearts made clean!
Nor by the washing of water,
For Judas was not sactified

Remember and meditate on
The beauty of that Passover
When servants sat down - and the king arose
To serve and minister to them
around the table on that night

Paint blood on the walls of your hearts
and you shall certainly be saved
Quickly remove - leaven from your bread
Let us sit and eat Passover
and go to the land of Canaan

Dear Breth'ren, People of God!
Sing thanksgiving unto the Son
Who offered Himself - and reconciled us
by his body and by his blood
and bless the father and spirit

Ba'usa of Mar Ephrem

The Son of God who carries
Seas in the palm of his hand
Pours water in a basin
to wash his disciple's feet
The one robed in majesty
ties a towel 'round his waist
He who is consuming fire
Bows before the disciples
The Watchers in heaven above
See him and stand in wonder

Seraphim clothed in lightning
Darken before his bright face
Hiding behind their six wings
Seeing the shining human
Bowing down his head before
Simon the Son of Jonah
Ranks of angels watch the earth
astonished by what they see

Cleanse me and purify me
O Lord who told your servants
"If I do not wash you clean
You shall have no part in me"
Lord, the spring of holiness
Wash to be which as snow
Bestow an inheritance
To the servant at your feast

Suthara Prayer

P:

Messiah, our God, who is true, who alone is perfect love, who by your love descended from heaven and walked the earth [ഭൂമിയിൽ വ്യാപരിച്ചു], who in your person [ക്നൂമ] revealed humility and the true path to perfection, who created all things by your eternal power, who sustains them by your word, the Giver of holiness and life, in that house where you held the feast with your disciples, in order to teach them humility by example, you rose from supper, tied a towel aroudn your waist, took water in a basin, and washed their feet. Teach us, O Lord, to likewise serve one another with the unity of love and genuine humility. Wash us of every stain of body and soul. Remove from us every bitterness, anger, jealousy, envy, immoderation [ദുൎവ്വാശി], conflict, corrupt talk and all evil deeds. O Lord, may we delight in the joy of this your festival, he satisfied by the fruits of your Gospel, and bear praise and thanksgiving to you, your father, and the Holy Spirit, hosho...

Ba'usa of Mar Aprem

Manna rains down in the camp
The Warriors of Israel
Cry "Manna Manna" aloud
in joy for this nourishment
The bread tastes like honeycomb
As they place it in their mouths
They recieve a renewed strength
To continue their journey
Then with certain steps they walk
Through the dry and barren land
The rock split and water poured
like a river behind them
They drank from it and recieved
freedom from their enslavement

Lord who redeemed those oppressed
Beneath the yoke of Satan
O Manna, the bread of life
who was sent down from heaven
What a mystery we see
within you at your table

Servants bathe and are made clean
In the river of your grace
They drink it and are nourished
and recieve blessed freedom
Give the joy of salvation
to us and lead us daily
Praise be to you forever
and your father and spirit
