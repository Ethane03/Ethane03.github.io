Opening Prayer

P:

O Lord our God, who neither slumbers nor sleeps, wake us from our deep slumber of sin, so that we may praise Your watchfulness over us. Immortal and living God, arouse us from the sleep of death and destruction, so that we may adore Your mercy. O Lord, who is the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit, because You alone are praised and glorified in heaven and on earth, make us also worthy to praise and worship You, together with all the majestic angelic hosts who in holiness, praise You in heaven.

C:

Amen

A great exchange took place when the
Only-begotten descended
He left the glory - and power of God
He bore spite and mock'ry for us
He suffered and was crucified

The immortal God was clothed in
robes of mortality for us
He chose to descend - even unto death
He shed his blood - and broke his flesh
On a cross atop Golgotha

If we daily humble ourselves
and cruficy lusts of the flesh
We shall put on the - power of his life
He did bear us upon the cross
We follow Him there and rejoice

God descended from glory.
Putting on a servant's form
he bore punishment and death.
He offered up his own flesh
as a sacrifice for sin

hated him and conspired
to tell falsehoods about him
so that he would be punished

Praise be to You, Son of God!
For you chose to deliver
us by suffring on the cross
Praise be to You who redeemed
us by the blood of your life!
