Anaphora of Mar Johannan

St. John the Evangelist

The Sign of Peace

Prayer of Peace

P:

Lord God Amighty, who are true love, invincible peace, hope that does not dissapoint, grant unto your servants who stand before your greatness, love, joy, tranquility, and unending peace. Make us all worthy to give eachother peace by the Holy Kiss that is pleasing to Your name, with purity of heart and sanctity of soul.

C:

Amen

P:

Peace be with you all.

C:

And with your spirit

D:

By the love of our Lord God, let us all give peace among one another with the Holy and Divine kiss.

Give the Kiss of Peace to one another. Afterwards, the congregation recites with bowed heads:

C:

Make us worthy, O Lord God, of this peace all the days of our lives. O our gracious Lord and God, having received this holy and divine peace, we bow our heads in your presence.

P:

O Lord, at this time when we have bowed the necks of our souls and bodies before your might, send us from the holy heights glorious grace, and a steady and irremovable blessing. May we give thanks and glorify you, your only son, and your holy spirit. Hosho...

C:

Amen

P:

O Lord, who at the end of the ages sent your beloved Son for our salvation, who delivered these holy and life-giving mysteries unto us, do not make us aliens us from this service. Turn not your holy face from us on account of the multitude of our sins. For you alone are holy, along with your only Son and the Holy Spirit. Hosho...

C:

Amen.

D:

Beloved brothers and sisters let us attend to this Holy Qurbana which is now offered before us by the hand of the priest, with commitment, reverence, humility, purity of heart, love, true faith and devotion. To God the Father, to whom all things belong, is offered this sacrifice of grace, peace and thanksgiving in a spirit of unity and concord by the priest for us all.

C:

This Qurbana is blessing, peace, and thanksgiving

The secret prayer of the priest as he removes the Shosappa from the Casa and the Pilasa

P:

This is the flintstone from which poured streams of water for the twelve tribes of Israel. This is the stone which was placed above the tomb of our Saviour.

The First Blessing (Rushma)

P:

The love of God the Father ✠ the grace of the only begotten Son ✠ and the communion and abiding presence of the Holy Spirit ✠ be with you all, dearly beloved, forever.

C:

And with your spirit.

The Dialogue

(The priest with uplifted arms)

P:

At this time, our thoughts, our hearts, and our mind must be in the heights at the right hand of God the Father, where the Messiah dwells.

C:

Truly they are with the Lord.

P:

To sing praises and venerate the Maker of all creation.

C:

Assuredly is good and right.

The priest bows his head and prays in secret:

P:

It is good and right for us to revere and give thanks to the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, one true God

Sanctus

Raising his voice

P:

The hosts above and the heaven in which they dwell praise you and exalt you, the fiery ones honor you with fear, the Cherubim bless you with trembling, the Seraphim with flashing feathers and beating wings fly one to another crying out, "Holy holy holy art thou Lord God Almighty", hallowing your mightyness. And we too praise you,

C:

Holy, Holy, Holy, Lord God Almighty
Thy praises fill heaven and earth
Hosanna in the highest!

Blessed is the one who has come
and is to come in the name of the Lord
Hosanna in the highest!

The priest bows his head and prays in secret:

P:

Thou, O King of worlds, truly are sacred and the sanctifier. And your Son, our Lord Jesus the Messiah, is holy. And the holy spirit, who is holy and who purifies your hidden mysteries, is holy. You made man from the earth, and set him in Paradise. When he transgressed your command and strayed, you did not cast him aside,but taught him the way by the Prophets. In the end, you sent your only Son into the world. He took flesh from the Holy Spirit and the Virgin Mary, and renewed your deteriorated image.

Words of Institution

Raising his voice

P:

When he willingly came for suffering, and bore the holy cross for our salvation, we too bread in his holy hands, before the gathering of his disciples, looked to heaven and gave thanks, blessed it, sanctified it, broke it, and gave it to his holy ones, saying, "Take and eat, this is my body, broken for your sake and for all those who believe in me, and given for the remission of sins and life eternal.

C:

Amen

P:

After His mystical supper, He took the cup, mixed of wine and water, gave thanks, blessed it, sanctified it, and gave it to the gathering of his disciples saying, "this is my blood. Drink from it all of you. It is poured out for the remission of sins forever and life eternal for all who believe in me.

C:

Amen

P:

When you commune in this mystery, make memory of me and proclaim My death until I come.

C:

O Lord, we remember Your death. We celebrate your resurrection. And we look forward to Your second coming. May your blessings be upon us all.

C:

Amen

Anamnesis

P:

At this time that we make remembrance of Your redemptive dispensation, O Messiah our God, we entreat and pray to Your goodness. When You come in your glory with Your holy angels, when you establish the throne of your Majesty, and You command the earth to bring forth all the dead to stand before You in fear and trembling; when You make lambs to stand at the right and the goats at the left and everyone looks to the reward which he deserves and the mansions which are reserved for him; Lord let us hear not from You that bitter and deadly word. Lord, do not say to us: Depart from Me, you cursed, and go to the fire." Lord, may we not be strangers to Your inheritance, and turn not your face from us. Look not on us with wrath. O Lord, let not the memory of our sins and iniquities enter your holy heart. Enter not into judgement with us. Do not treat us as the hopeless. Do not oppose us like enemies...

C:

Have mercy upon us, O God the omnipotent Father. O Lord God, we praise you, we honor you, we glorify you. O Good One, we appeal to you that you might have mercy on us.

C:

Amen

P:

O Lord, we, your weak and sinful servants, have recieved your grace, and celebrate your loving-kindness for all and unto all

Epiclesis

D:

My beloved, how solemn is this moment and how terrible this hour, in which the Spirit of Holiness and Life descends in majesty from the highest heavens and abideth upon the holy mystery placed before us; and sanctifieth it. Let us all stand with fear and reverence and pray that peace may be with us and good will towards us all

C:

May peace be with us and tranquillity to all of us

P:

(In secret) Have mercy upon me, O Lord, and send upon me and upon this Qurbana which has been offered Your Holy Spirit Who makes perfect all the Mysteries of your Church

P:

(Raising his voice) Answer unto me, O Lord. Answer unto me, O Lord. Answer unto me, O Lord. By Your grace, have mercy upon us.

C:

Kurie-elaison Kurie-elaison Kurie-elaison

P:

May the Holy Spirit descend and sanctify this bread that it may be the body of our Lord Jesus Christ.

C:

Amen.

P:

May the Holy Spirit descend and sanctify this cup which is prepared that it may be the blood of our Lord Jesus Christ.

C:

Amen.

P:

C:

Amen.

The Great Intercession

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

The Second Blessing (Rushma)

P:

The blessing of our great God and Savior Jesus Christ ✠ be with you all, forever.

C:

And with your spirit.

The Breaking of the Bread

The priest breaks the bread and blesses it. Then he prays in secret:

P:

O Lord God the Messiah, in Jerusalem...

Raising his voice

P:

O God who accepts our prayers and answers our requests, we pray before you this Lord's Prayer which was taught to us by your only-begotten and holy Son. With a broken heart we cry aloud and pray, "Our Father, who art in Heaven"

C:

Our Father who art in heaven...

P:

C:

Amen. Before we recieve these holy mysteries offered before us, O merciful Lord God, we bow our heads in your presence.

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

P:

C:

Amen

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

The Third Blessing (Rushma)

P:

May the grace, mercy and blessings of the Holy and Glorious Trinity, uncreated, ✠ self-existent, eternal, ✠ adorable and one in essence ✠ be with you all dearly beloved forever.

C:

And with your Spirit. O Holy and Glorious Trinity, have mercy upon us.

Here follow the normal liturgy from "Let us hold ourselves in reverance and awe" until the Prayer of Thanksgiving

P:

Peace be with you all

C:

And with your Spirit

P:

C:

Amen

Recite the normal liturgy from the Huthama to the end
