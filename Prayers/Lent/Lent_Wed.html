Wednesday Prayers

O our Messiah, who offered yourself on the cross, who remits our sins by thy sacrifice, who gives us life by your own death, who raises us by thy resurrection, accept our prayers and petitions, and forgive our sins and our iniquities. O Lord, remove all division and arguments and self-seeking from our church that you redeemed by your holy blood. And may you decorate it with love, with peace, with unity, and with knowledge of the three-in-one God. Hosho vab’kula suban l’olmeen.

Amen

Psalm 56

Have mercy on me, O God,

for my enemies are hounding me

all day long they assault and oppress me

They hound me all the day long

truly there are many who fight against me, O Most High

Whenever I am afraid,

I will put my trust in you.

In God, whose word I praise,

in God I trust and will not be afraid,

for what can flesh do to me?

All day long they damage my cause

their only thought is to do me evil.

They band together; they lie in wait;

they spy upon my footsteps;

because they seek my life

Shall they escape despite their wickedness?

O God, in your anger, cast down the peoples

You have noted my lamentation;

put my tears into your bottle

are they not recorded in your book?

Whenever I call upon you, my enemies will be put to flight;

this I know, for God is on my side.

In God the Lord, whose word I praise,

in God I trust and will not be afraid,

for what can mortals do to me?

I am bound by the vow I made to you, O God;

I will present to you thank-offerings;

For you have rescued my soul from death and my feet

from stumbling,

that I may walk before God in the light of the living.

 

 

 

Enyana

(Nombu orthodox Monday sapro)

Messiah who by – His great mercies
Fasted and shamed the devil
And defeated his power
We bow to you; Lord, have mercy!

Christ our God who for – our salvation
Kept the fast like one in need
And gave us its pleasant yoke
We bow to you; Lord, have mercy!

Christ who taught the Fast – to His warriors
In order to fight Satan
with fasting and heartfelt prayer
We bow to you; Lord, have mercy!

Make us worthy, Lord – Lover of Man
To keep Lent with discernment
And to be absolved of sins
We bow to you; Lord, have mercy!

Lord, You have spoken – through the prophets
“This is the fast I desire:
Brother shall love his brother”
We bow to you; Lord, have mercy!

O God, by your grace – You have granted
The fast’s richness to us all
May we fast with purity
We bow to you; Lord, have mercy!

Promeon

O Lord, you are worshipped by ranks clothed in tongues of fire, and glorified by all the holy angels. At this time of prayer, and all the days of our lives, thou art worthy of worship and honor and glory. Hosho vab’kula suban l’olmeen.

Amen

Sedra

O good and benevolent God, take not your grace from your church that you rescued by your high and mighty hand. O Lord, as you have demonstrated your wrath, do also with your mercy. Let not the good things of the world fall prey to desert winds and troubles and hardships. Take us away from evil times and difficulties. May you command the needed times to us from your presence. Answer unto us O Lord. Do not delay! See the worrisome state of your people. Have mercy on the souls of us all. Fulfill the needs of the lost, the poor, and the widows, for they ever call upon you, they ever wait upon you. O God, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, we praise you. Hosho vab’kula suban l’olmeen.

Amen

Qolo

As it is written, at an unthought-of time the day of our Lord shall come upon the earth. Let us become great by repentance. Let us collect oil for our lamps, so that when he comes, we may come beside him, not hearing the words “Depart from me, cursed ones, and go down into the place of fire, I never knew you”

Sinners, repent of unrighteousness, and appeal with grief to the one who has opened his treasury, that he may give gifts unto the needy. Let us not fall asleep like the foolish ones who gathered with their lamps without collecting oil, but rather imitate the wise ones who took care of their oil in jars, and joined the bridegroom in the wedding hall.

O Great Judge of judges, our Lord, do not lower our heads at the judgement place. Let us not enter into Hades.

Turn us back toward your holy wisdom. May we take refuge in you and become virtuous by you. Save us from Hades by the mercy of your grace and let us become inheritors of your Kingdom.

O Lord, accept our prayer. Be at peace with us who have angered you. Do not turn your face from the servant who calls upon you with faith. Grow your peace and goodwill in all corners of the world. Blot out arguments and strife from the Holy church and her children. Font of mercy, praise be to your mercy!

O God who bent down your strength so that our race might not be destroyed, who freed us from slavery to sin by your passion, have mercy on your people who cry out to you in sadness. Give peace and unity to them that they might be glad and rejoice, and praise you, your Father, and the Holy Spirit.

Lord have mercy upon us and help us (Moryo rahem alain w’adarain)

Beloved, let us gather with the righteous
Who ever pleased God
Let us plead with the Lord, and sing like David,
“I wake at night and praise your righteous judgement,
O Lord, have mercy”

Those who fast, take care in receiving the Fast
            which strengthens all things
May body and soul be cleansed from evil
And our fast please God and be for the pardon
            Of our debts and sins

How swift are the wings of the fast which makes us
            Ascend to the heights!
Those who fast and do not mix falsehood with it
Are mingled with the spirituals and graze on
            The bliss of heaven

We knock at Your door and we beg for mercy
            From your treasure-house
We are all sinners who have strayed from Your way
Grant that we confess and be free from our sins
            And live in Your grace

Bless’d is He, the Sacrifice on Golgotha
who freed us mortals
From the devil and from slavery to sin
Send the Spirit, who gives life to the spirits
of those you redeemed

Let us never ask Abraham for water
O Living Water!
We call to you, “Let us drink the desert,
Open the gate of your Kingdom unto us
Send us not to fire”

Lord have mercy upon us and help us (Moryo rahem alain w’adarain)

Concluding Prayer

O Lord, (make this morning a morning of blessing and comfort and the dawn of a joyful day), on this day make haste to save me from evil men, from cheating neighbors, from the attacks of Satan,  from evil times, from destructive sights, from harm, from false words, from idle conversation, from unknown errors, from the opposition of the enemy, from impure commerce, and from abominable emotions. O my Lord, give me a disciplined body, a pure tongue, and controlled thoughts. May you give to me all the things that are helpful to my spirit, and pleasing to your lordship. May you nourish me with sufficient food, useful riches, and protection of my soul. O my Lord, make me worthy so that I may hate violence, leave behind wickedness, to abstain from sin, to be removed from evil, to be obedient to my parents, to be favorable to my friends, to respect my brothers and sisters, and to glorify your holy name in all that I do. Amen.
