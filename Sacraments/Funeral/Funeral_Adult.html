Opening Prayer

P:

God, Lord of every life, when you desired to redeem our race from the eternal wretchedness that results from sin, Your only son put on the garments of humanity, He died and was buried, on the third day He broke the bonds of death and rose to life, and from our mortal and sinful bodies He has prepared a body in which we might experience eternal life in the resurrection. We remember all these things, and thereby give thanks to You. O Lord, this dear one has now come to the end of the days of his/her wretched life. But Your desire is always for our goodness. O Lord, we magnify Your Holy name for every mercy that you worked in him/her, and for taking him/her from this world filled with hardship and grief. May you make each of us worthy, by your Holy Spirit, to be prepared to come to your side at your call, with peace of mind in the Messiah. Be gracious to us that we too might inherit eternal salvation with him/her and all your faithful.

Sedra

P:

O Lord God, Mighty, Warrior, Terrible, who rules over all things, who created all things by His word, who undoes all things by His command, who brings forth life by His will and the end by his power, who is the breath of all things, return [തിരിയുക] over us, your weak and mortal servants. Aid us with you holy hosts until we depart from our short stay in the foreign land of this strange life, and reach heavenly Jerusalem. When the angels blow the horn, great armies cry out, and the hosts of Cherubim proclaim the power of your fearfulness on the last day of your appearance, when your majesty dawns, your rule is proclaimed, Your Kingdom is revealed, your strength is made known, and your fearfulness rains down on all people, then fallen are the kingdoms! Undone are the lordships! Gone is the number of months! Ended are day and night! All creation is reduced to nothing. Then the heavens tremble. The sun darkens. The moon and stars put on dark clouds. Then you come in awfulness. You are seen in light. You set all borders and parts in trembling and shock. Then do the righteous rejoice. The saints are glad. All the children of light set out to greet your arrival. The wicked are ashamed. The unfaithful grieve. The throne of your honor is established. Your terrible voice gathers all peoples. Justice strikes fear. The fire rages. The sword is sharpened. The darkness rises. Destruction and agony come swiftly. The gnashing of teeth is heard. The good are divided. The sinners are judged. The works of every person are examined publicly. O Lord God, merciful and abundant in blessings, we appeal to you: there in that hour make haste to save us from fear and torment. O Lord, have mercy on us because of your love for mankind. Look not on the hateful things we have done. Remember not the sins we have commited. Condemn us not to punishment in your terrible judgement. Leave us not in despair. Make us not heirs of fire. Bar us not from your grace. May you spread the wings of your mercy and cover us. Make us worthy of your kingdom. O Judge, who is Righteous, True, Impartial, who gives to each according to their deeds, May we enter with you into the bridal chamber, with gladness and rejoicing, and sing praise for your grace and mercies. To you belongs the praise, along with your Father and your Holy Spirit. Hasha...

O God, Eternal and Lord above tens of thousands, by your divine wisdom and grace you took our first father Adam and formed him from the dirt, blew the breath of life and reason into Him, put him in authority over all, set him in the Paradise of Eden and allowed him to eat of every tree within it, and though you bade him to not eat of the tree of knowledge, he transgressed that command, eating from the forbidden tree, and because of this his disobediance he became an outsider to your righteousness and from the Paradise of joys he was cast down to the place of vexation, and he earned suffering and death for him and for us, his children.
